FAQs

PSW Program — FAQs

Everything you need to know about the Personal Support Worker program.

The Personal Support Worker (PSW) program is a career-focused certificate program that prepares you to provide personal care and support to seniors, individuals with disabilities and others in long-term care, community and home-care settings across Ontario.
Ontario Permanent Residents and Canadian Citizens may qualify for Ontario government education funding, including grants and student-loan support, which can cover up to 100% of tuition. Work Permit Holders and Protected Refugees can enrol through self-funded options. Call us to check your eligibility.
The PSW program can be completed in approximately 6 months, including both the academic component and a clinical placement in a real healthcare setting.
No prior experience is required. The program is beginner-friendly and designed to build your skills from the ground up, making it ideal for career changers, newcomers to Canada and first-time learners.
Touch Abroad assists with selecting the right program, checking government-funding eligibility, completing the college application, enrolment and ongoing student support — all free as part of our end-to-end service.
