FAQs

Cyber Security — FAQs

Everything you need to know about the Cyber Security program.

The Cyber Security program prepares you for an entry-level career protecting computer networks, systems and data. You'll build practical, job-ready skills in areas such as network security, threat detection, security operations and incident response — with training aligned to what Ontario employers are hiring for.
Eligible Ontario Permanent Residents and Canadian Citizens may qualify for government grants and student-loan support that can cover part or all of tuition. Work Permit Holders and Protected Refugees can enrol through self-funded options. Call us at +1 (289) 969-7018 and we'll confirm your eligibility for free.
No formal IT background is required. The program is designed to build your skills from the ground up, so it's a strong fit for career changers and newcomers — though general comfort with computers is helpful. We'll talk through your starting point during your free consultation.
Cyber Security programs are typically mapped toward industry-recognised certifications. Because the exact certifications depend on the specific college program, we'll confirm which ones your chosen program prepares you for before you enrol, so there are no surprises.
Graduates start in roles such as security analyst, SOC (Security Operations Centre) analyst, IT security technician and cybersecurity support specialist — working in IT teams, managed security providers, financial services, government and virtually any organisation that needs to protect its data.
Touch Abroad provides complete support — helping you choose the right program, checking your funding eligibility, completing the college application and finalising enrolment — all free as part of our end-to-end service.
